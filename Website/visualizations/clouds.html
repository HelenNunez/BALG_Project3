Latitude and Cloudiness

This plot depicts the reported cloudiness from and the latitudes of different cities across the world. I notice significant clustering at the bottom and top of the map, representing 0% and 100% cloudiness ratings, indicating that a lot of location experience full sun or full clouds. However, latitude does not clearly play a part.

Cloudiness does appear to have some clustering at the low and high levels, indicating that there may be some correlation between cloudiness an latitude (cloudier within 10 degrees of the equator and sunnier 20 to 40 degrees away?), but no conclusions can be drawn without further investigations.

Southern Latitude cloudiness does show significance at a 95% confidence level, but given the slight nature, it might be worth investigating if there were enough data points at the furthest south temperatures. R-squared values indicate that the linear fit was not a particularly good predictor for expected temperatures.
